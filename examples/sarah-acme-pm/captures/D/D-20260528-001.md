## Decision

Q3 mobile work prioritizes 7-day retention. Acquisition stays at maintenance level — no new growth experiments until the activation rate is above 50%.

## Rationale

Current 7-day retention sits at 19%. Industry median for our segment is 31%. Pouring more top-of-funnel into a leaky bucket compounds the problem and obscures the underlying activation issue.

## Alternatives considered

- **Split focus (retention + acquisition):** rejected because we have one engineer dedicated to mobile this quarter; splitting that = neither ships.
- **Defer to Q4:** rejected because the Q3 board update will surface the retention number; we want to be already-working-on-it, not promising-to-look-into-it.

## Reversibility

Medium. If the activation work doesn't move the needle in 6-8 weeks, we can pivot mid-quarter back to acquisition. Reframing is easy; the engineering work doesn't lock us in.

## Decided

Sarah + Alex, in 1:1 on 2026-05-28. Documented in OKR proposal draft.
